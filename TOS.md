## 3. Data Usage and Analysis

### 3.1 LLM Model Training and Automated Assessment
The SSPE Department uses student data to train and improve a Large Language Model (LLM) system designed to enhance educational outcomes. Specifically, your lesson plans and unit plans may be used for:

#### 3.1.1 Automated Suggestions and Improvements
The LLM model analyzes submitted plans to automatically suggest:
- Improvements and enhancements to lesson content
- Additional details that may have been missed
- Best practices and pedagogical recommendations

#### 3.1.2 Automated Feedback and Quality Assurance
The system provides automated feedback by:
- Identifying critical logical inconsistencies in lesson plans
- Detecting timing and scheduling conflicts
- Flagging potential safety concerns or issues
- Highlighting areas that may require attention

#### 3.1.3 Automated Grading
The LLM model automatically assigns preliminary grades to submitted work based on:
- Completeness and quality of the lesson plan
- Adherence to educational standards and requirements
- Identification of critical issues or inconsistencies

**Important:** All automatically assigned grades are subject to manual review and approval by qualified human staff members before being finalized and locked into students' academic records. The automated grade serves as a preliminary assessment tool to assist educators in the review process.

### 3.2 Data Display
Your data is collected and stored so that it can be retrieved and displayed to you when you request access to your lesson plans, unit plans, or other stored information.
